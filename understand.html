Understand

A product person needs to know how their product is being used. For this website I use a combination of Google Analytics, Tag Manager and Hotjar.

Google Analytics

It was end time I dedicated the time to take the Google Analytics Individual Qualification Exam. The Google Academy courses helped put a fresh perspective on the depth and breadth of analysis you could do with Analytics. My future plans are to test the Google Analytics A/B testing feature with my side project.

Google Tag Manager

Setting up goals that show on your Analytics Dashboard is much much easier with Tag manager. Later today (13th May 2019) I'm gonna add a few tags in Tag Manager that are going to fire in Analytics. For example I'll be tracking CV downloads from the resources page, clicks on the links to my blog, as well as there'll be a few goals related to how people nagivate through the website. If you're interested, check out this page again around midnight today for it's updated version. You know, there's only as many hours in the day one can work on their portfolio :)

Hotjar

I use Hotjar for their scrollmaps and heatmaps. Scroll maps are my favourite. It's very useful to know on which pages people just don't bother scrolling to the bottom. That might be a signal to change or reduce some of the content. Btw the feedback tag you see on the right of your screen also comes with Hotjar. You can give it a try!
